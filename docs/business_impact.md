# Business Impact Analysis

## Quantified Revenue Impact

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Churned Customers | 1,869 |
| Monthly Revenue Lost | $139,131 |
| Annual Revenue Lost | $1,669,572 |
| Avg Monthly Charge (Churned) | $74.44 |
| Avg Monthly Charge (Retained) | $61.27 |
| Revenue Premium of Churned Customers | +21% higher monthly charges |

**Critical insight:** Churned customers actually pay more per month than retained customers. This means the revenue loss from churn is even higher than the customer count alone would suggest.

## Customer Acquisition Cost Context

Industry benchmark: Acquiring a new telecom customer costs 5–7x more than retaining an existing one.

- If CAC = $300/customer: Replacing 1,869 churned customers costs ~$560,700–$841,050 in acquisition spend
- Total annual cost of churn (revenue loss + acquisition replacement): $2.2M–$2.5M

## Churn Risk Segments — Business Priority

### Segment 1: Month-to-Month Fiber Optic (Critical Risk)
- Size: ~1,400 customers
- Churn rate: ~50%+
- Monthly revenue at risk: ~$120,000
- Action: Immediate outreach, contract conversion offer, service quality check

### Segment 2: Month-to-Month, Tenure < 18 Months (High Risk)
- Size: ~1,800 customers
- Churn rate: ~45%
- Monthly revenue at risk: ~$100,000
- Action: Early tenure retention sequence, loyalty incentives

### Segment 3: Senior Citizens on Month-to-Month (High Risk)
- Size: ~400 customers
- Churn rate: ~50%+
- Action: Simplified plan, dedicated support line

## Long-Term Impact of Retention

If churn rate is reduced from 26.5% to 20% (achievable with program):
- Customers retained additionally: ~460/year
- Annual revenue protected: ~$411,000
- 5-year cumulative impact: ~$2M+ in retained revenue
