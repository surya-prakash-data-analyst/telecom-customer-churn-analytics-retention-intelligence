## Bottom Line Up Front

Our telecom business is losing **$1.67M annually** from customer churn. The data shows this is largely preventable. Three segments account for the majority of loss: month-to-month contract customers, fiber optic service users, and senior citizens. Targeted retention programs in these areas can protect an estimated $1M+ in annual recurring revenue.

## What the Data Tells Us

**Customers leave because:**
1. They are not locked into contracts (month-to-month = maximum flexibility to leave)
2. Fiber optic service may not be delivering value proportional to its cost
3. They lack support services (no tech support, no security) = no product "stickiness"
4. They are in their first 1.5 years and have not built loyalty yet

**Customers stay because:**
- Long-term contracts (two-year: only 2.8% churn)
- They have been customers for 5+ years (loyalty effect)
- They have multiple services (security, backup, support) bundled
